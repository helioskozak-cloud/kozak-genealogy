## Research Summary

### What was proven

1. **Senator John J. Relihan Jr. (Dooley/Duley branch) died in office in 1966.** Wikipedia's article on Connecticut's 21st State Senate District confirms: John J. Relihan (Democrat, district home: Bridgeport) served 1959–1966 and "Died in office." Source: Hartford Courant, December 16, 1966, p. 30. This gives us his approximate death date (mid-December 1966) and closes his career. Spouse and children remain unknown.

2. **Ann Relihan Lang obituary fully recovered** (snippet confirmed all key fields): She was "daughter of John and Ann Duley Relihan." Preceded in death by brothers "John, Thomas, and Edward Relihan." Survived by husband Frank Lang; sons Frank O. Lang III + wife Julie, Matthew G. Lang + wife Brenda; daughter Carol Barnes + husband Doug; 7 grandchildren; sister Margaret Melott. Funeral: Assumption Catholic Church, Woodbridge CT. Burial: Northwest Cemetery, Woodbridge CT. This is Tier 1 confirmation for the Dooley/Duley branch structure.

3. **Margaret Relihan Mellott is in the Dooley/Duley branch.** Ann Lang's 2008 obituary explicitly lists "Sister, Margaret Melott" as a survivor. This resolves the conflation question — Margaret Relihan Mellott is Ann Lang's blood sister, therefore a child of John Relihan + Ann Duley Relihan. The Lauralton Hall Class of 1951 listing alongside McNamara-branch girls is a coincidence of era and school, not evidence of branch membership.

4. **The Willig Funeral Home Margaret R. Mellott (b. May 13, 1927, d. December 4, 2022)** is likely the same Margaret Relihan Mellott: she would have been 81 at Ann Lang's death in 2008 (consistent with surviving), and born 1927 → Lauralton Hall graduation ~1944-1951 range (consistent with Class of 1951 if she took time off or started late). This needs one more confirming source but is strongly probable.

5. **All three brothers (John, Thomas, Edward) predeceased Ann Lang (d. Dec 22, 2008).** Therefore: Senator John J. Relihan Jr. died before Dec 22, 2008 (Wikipedia says 1966); Thomas Peter Relihan died before Dec 22, 2008; Edward A. Relihan died before Dec 22, 2008.

6. **Msgr. G. Thomas Burns confirmed family line:** Father McGivney → Rose McGivney Finn → Zita Finn Burns → Msgr. G. Thomas Burns. Burns's obituary (Leonardis Memorial Home) states: "His maternal grandmother was Rose McGivney (Finn), Michael's sister." Parents: Gerald T. Burns + Zita (Finn) Burns of West Orange NJ. Three priest-uncles: Msgr. Leo M. Finn, Francis J. Finn, Vincent E. Finn.

7. **Patricia Finn Moriarty confirmed Ransom line:** Born June 7, 1933, Waterbury CT. Daughter of Dr. Alfred J. Finn + Elizabeth (Meuten) Finn. Grandniece of Father McGivney. Siblings: sister Marcia Rice (= Marcia Finn Rice), brothers Dr. Mark L. Finn, Dr. Alfred J. Finn Jr., Billy Finn (deceased). Patricia mentions cousin "Zita (Burns) O'Brian" (Monsignor Burns's sister), confirming both families descend from Rose McGivney Finn. Patricia's children include Elizabeth Moriarty Clouart, Maureen Moriarty Laughnane, Mark, Paul, Timothy, Thomas, William Moriarty.

8. **Margaret Ransom = great-grandniece through Dr. Alfred J. Finn line.** The confirmed chain: Father McGivney → Rose McGivney Finn → Dr. Alfred J. Finn → Marcia Finn Rice → Margaret Ransom. Her husband Bob Ransom is administrator of the cause for McGivney's sainthood. Sons Michael and Kevin Ransom. Unveiled McGivney statue at St. Patrick's Cathedral NYC, February 2025, alongside John Walshe and his sons Hugh and Jake.

9. **Marianna Phillips confirmed as McGivney's great-grandniece via Margaret McGivney Dwyer (different line from Walshe/Finn).** Aleteia article: granddaughter of Margaret McGivney Dwyer (Father McGivney's sister). Father died young; raised with help from aunt Marguerite Dwyer (born after McGivney died). Grew up in Waterbury CT. Husband Jack (Lutheran convert 1987). Age 74 in Oct 2020.

10. **John Walshe confirmed line:** Father McGivney → Rose McGivney Finn → [Finn child, unnamed] → John Walshe. His granduncle was Msgr. Leo Finn (= he was taught to drive by Leo Finn in 1960). First cousin is Joseph Finn (also attended beatification 2020). Sons Hugh Walshe and Jake Walshe (confirmed at statue unveiling Feb 2025 per mokofc.org article).

### What was disproven

- **No Relihan burials indexed at St. Michael's Cemetery Stratford** in the CT Genweb transcription (only McNamara entries found). This does not disprove Joan B. Relihan's statement that parents are buried there — the transcription may be incomplete or those records may post-date the index.

- **No direct genealogical bridge between any Relihan and any McGivney family member was found.** The surnames Walshe, Finn, Burns, Ransom, Dwyer, Phillips — none appear as spouses or relatives in any Relihan-side obituary or record searched.

## Final Conclusion

**No proven connection found, and current leads are proximity only.**

All research across 10 priority targets and extensive web searching found no documentary evidence of a genealogical connection between Grant Kozak's maternal Relihan line and the family of Blessed Father Michael J. McGivney.

**What was found on the McGivney side:** A well-documented extended family descending from two of McGivney's sisters — Rose McGivney Finn (giving rise to the Walshe, Finn, Burns, Ransom, Moriarty, and Rice lines) and Margaret McGivney Dwyer (giving rise to the Phillips line). All these families are concentrated in the Waterbury/Bridgeport/Hartford CT Catholic corridor.

**What was found on the Relihan side:** The Dooley/Duley branch (Grant's line) is now better documented: John Relihan + Ann Duley Relihan had children Thomas Peter (Grant's grandfather), Senator John J. (died in office Dec 1966), Edward A. (died ~May 1989), Ann Relihan Lang (d. Dec 2008), and Margaret Relihan Mellott (d. Dec 2022). All were Bridgeport CT Irish Catholics attending Assumption Church, Woodbridge and Northwest Cemetery, Woodbridge.

**Why no bridge has been found:** Despite both families being Irish Catholic in the same geographic corridor, no marriage record, obituary, or public source places any Relihan in a family relationship with any McGivney descendant. The "bridge surnames" (Walshe, Finn, Burns, Dwyer, Phillips, Ransom) do not appear as spouses or in-laws in any Relihan-side document found.

**The single most decisive record** that could either confirm or close this question is the obituary of Senator John J. Relihan Jr. from the Hartford Courant, approximately December 15-20, 1966. He died in office at approximately age 38-39, potentially leaving young children who would be in their late 50s/60s today. If any of his children married into a McGivney-descendant family, that would constitute a genuine bridge. This record requires physical access to Hartford Courant microfilm at the Connecticut State Library, 231 Capitol Ave, Hartford CT 06106.
