Session — Jul 3 2026 Part 23 (S102 · Andrew McGivney emigrated Aug 1833 · Catherine McCabe Pottlebrack · Bernard McGivney b.1830 son of Andrew · 1827 Bernard RESOLVED)

Andrew McGivney's emigration fully documented — from RootsWeb Crosserlough page (S102): Andrew McGivney (b.~1800/1801, Creenow) worked as a farm hand in Pottlebrack townland (neighboring Creenow in Crosserlough Parish), where he met and married Catherine McCabe (b.~1804 Pottlebrack, daughter of Dennis McCabe). Bernard McGivney (b.~1830 County Cavan) = Andrew's first son, born before emigration: Bernard McGivney (b.~1830, County Cavan) is confirmed as the son of Andrew McGivney + Catherine McCabe (S102). Bernard married Ann Lawless (b.~1835 Ireland). Their daughter: Catherine McGivney (b.1863 Malone, Franklin County, NY). Bernard was born ~3 years before Andrew and Catherine emigrated (August 1833), so he was born in County Cavan and traveled to the US as an infant. Bernard was thus the first child of Andrew and Catherine — born in Creanew/Pottlebrack area of Crosserlough Parish. The RootsWeb source notes Andrew had multiple children (some sources say 9 total), but only Ann (b.1847 Malone NY) and Bernard (b.~1830 Cavan) are specifically identified. Source: S102.

CRITICAL: 1827 "Bernard → Bryan" RESOLVED — Bernard was Andrew's deceased BROTHER: The 1827 Creanew estate record states Bryan McGivney took over 27 acres previously owned by Bernard (S90). Andrew's son Bernard was born ~1830 — AFTER 1827 — so he cannot be the 1827 Bernard. The 1827 Bernard was Patrick McGivney's son (b.~1803–1808), Andrew's younger brother, who held land in Creenow briefly before dying young. Bryan (another of Patrick's sons) then inherited Bernard's 27 acres in 1827. Andrew honored his deceased brother by naming his own firstborn son Bernard (b.~1830 Cavan). This follows the classic Irish naming pattern of naming children after recently deceased siblings. The family sequence in Creenow: Patrick (b.~1782) had sons Andrew (b.~1801), Bernard (~b.1803, died pre-1827), Bryan (~b.1808), Michael (~b.1810), Thomas (~b.1813), Nicholas Jr. (b.~1820) — six sons, consistent with 4 independent tenants in 1828 Tithe (Andrew, Bryan, Michael, Thomas; Bernard dead, Nicholas too young). Source: S102 + S90 combined analysis.

Patrick McGivney Sr.'s parents — CONFIRMED Jul 3 2026 Confirmed S90
Answer (confirmed Jul 3 2026 · S90)
Father: John McGivney (Derrylahan/Drumbee townland, Crosserlough Parish, County Cavan). Mother: Bridget Gibney (per Ancestry.com genealogy record for Patrick McGivney b.1832 Drumbee d.1878 Waterbury CT). WikiTree biography text independently names father as John McGivney but gives mother as "Mary Sheridan" — mother's maiden name is disputed, father's name John McGivney is consistent across all sources. Patrick was born in Drumbee townland, Crosserlough Parish, County Cavan (~1832). He and Mary Lynch emigrated ~1849–1850, married Waterbury CT Nov 22 1850, had 13 children (7 survived).

Why this matters for the Peter-Patrick brothers hypothesis: Peter McGivney Sr.'s confirmed father is Bryan McGivney in Creenow townland (same Crosserlough Parish). John McGivney (Drumbee/Derrylahan) and Bryan McGivney (Creenow) are BOTH documented in Griffith's Valuation for Crosserlough Parish ~1847–1864. In a small rural Irish parish, same-surname families across neighboring townlands are almost certainly related — Bryan and John McGivney were likely brothers or first cousins, making Peter McGivney Sr. and Patrick McGivney Sr. brothers or first cousins.
